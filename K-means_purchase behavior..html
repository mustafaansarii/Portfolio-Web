Customer Segmentation with K-Means Clustering

The aim of this project is to apply K-Means clustering to segment customers based on their purchase behavior.

Highlights:

  • Objective: Utilized K-Means clustering to segment customers based on their purchase behavior.
  • Dataset: Employed a customer purchase dataset to extract meaningful insights.
  • Methodology: Implemented preprocessing techniques and the K-Means clustering algorithm.
  • Insights: Derived actionable insights for targeted marketing strategies and personalized customer experiences.
  • Technologies: Utilized Python for data analysis, along with libraries such as Pandas, NumPy, Matplotlib, and Scikit-Learn.
  • Problem-Solving: Overcame challenges in data preprocessing and model optimization to ensure accurate segmentation.
  • Future Directions: Identified opportunities for further analysis and improvement in customer segmentation techniques.

    • Project Description: Customer segmentation is crucial for businesses aiming to understand their customer base and tailor marketing strategies accordingly. In this project, I leveraged K-Means clustering, a popular unsupervised learning algorithm, to segment customers based on their purchase behavior.

    Data Collection: Gathered a dataset from kaggle containing customer purchase information, including variables such as purchase frequency, monetary value of purchases, and types of products purchased.

    Data Preprocessing:
  • Handling Missing Values: Addressed any missing values in the dataset, employing techniques such as imputation or removal.
  • Outlier Detection: Identified and handled outliers in the data to prevent them from skewing clustering results.
  • Data Standardization: Standardized numerical features to ensure all variables were on the same scale.

    • Feature Selection: Chose relevant features that captured essential aspects of customer purchase behavior, such as frequency, recency, and monetary value.
